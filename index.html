Enhanced absorption performance of triethanolamine — bio-derived amino acid systems: Integrating experimental design and machine learning prediction

Bita Valadian, Peyvand Valeh-e-Sheyda, Amir Mehdi Parviz
DOI: 10.1016/j.ceja.2026.101132 Open Access
Novel TEA-arginine green solvent for enhanced CO₂ capture
Threefold enhancement over MDEA benchmark (306 vs. 107 kmol/h·m³)
Gas flow rate as dominant parameter — 61.7% contribution
Random Forest model accuracy R² > 0.988
This study introduces a hybrid solvent combining TEA with bio-derived l-arginine within a microfluidic contactor. The TEA-Arg system achieved 306 kmol/h·m³ volumetric molar flux (73% capture efficiency) — a threefold enhancement over MDEA. Random Forest models accurately predicted key mass transfer parameters (R² > 0.988), enabling optimized CO₂ capture process design.
